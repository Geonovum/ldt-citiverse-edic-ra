# EDIC Architecture

    This is a first summary of content for this chapter. It has not been reviewed or can be considered stable as of yet...
</aside>


The LDT CitiVERSE EDIC is driven by European Digital Decade values. It tackles real challenges around governance, sustainability, intellectual property and citizen-centricity through a federated, interoperable architecture.
This architecture is built on a solid set of capabilities covering Digital Twins, Data, AI and Community tools — and is shaped by strong requirements for interoperability, security, deployment flexibility, usability, operational resilience and documentation.

## Purpose

The motivation view explains the reasoning behind the LDT CitiVERSE EDIC reference architecture. It connects five key elements into a single, coherent architecture vision:

- European Digital Decade values
- EDIC strategic drivers
- Architecture principles
- Required capabilities
- Technical and organisational requirements

## Core Values (Digital Decade 2030)

The architecture is built on the values of the Digital Decade 2030:

- People at the Centre
- Participation
- Freedom of Choice
- Safety and Security
- Sustainability
- Solidarity and Inclusion

These values form the normative foundation of the entire architecture.

## EDIC Strategic Drivers

Four strategic drivers explain why the EDIC is being established and what challenges it needs to address:

- Citizen-centric approach – putting citizens first in every design decision
- Intellectual Property Management – handling ownership and rights across partners
- Multi-layer Governance – coordinating across different levels of authority
- Self-Sustainability Models – ensuring the EDIC can sustain itself over time

## Architecture Principles

The EDIC draws on the eGovERA architecture framework and adds CitiVERSE-specific principles:

| Principle | Description| 
| --- | --- |
| Federated | No single point of control; distributed by design |
| API Abstraction | Services exposed through standardised interfaces |
| Interoperability by Design | Systems work together from the ground up |
| Shared Data | Data is accessible across the ecosystem |

These principles guide every architecture decision that follows.

## Goals

The architecture targets five key goals:

- Public reporting of LDT Toolbox requirements
- Compliance with the EU AI Act
- Intellectual Property Management
- Multi-layer Governance
- Self-Sustainability Models

These goals translate the strategic drivers into measurable outcomes.

## Core Capabilities

The architecture combines capabilities from both eGovERA and the LDT CitiVERSE initiative.

__eGovERA Capabilities:__

- Digital Twin
- Knowledge Management
- Data Management
- Artificial Intelligence

__LDT CitiVERSE Capabilities:__

- AI
- LDT Computational Models
- LDT Data
- LDT Foundation
- Community Enablement
- LDT Visualisation

Together, these capabilities form the functional building blocks needed to bring the EDIC vision to life.
